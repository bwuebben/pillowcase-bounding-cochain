\title[Trace-free characters and instanton gradings for $2$-bridge and $(3,n)$ knots]
{Trace-free $\SU$ characters and $\Z/4$ instanton gradings\\ for two-bridge and $(3,n)$-torus knots}

\author{Bernd J. Wuebben}
\date{July 13, 2026}

\begin{abstract}
We assemble, and where possible independently verify, the representation-theoretic data underlying
the pillowcase (symplectic) side of the Atiyah--Floer conjecture for knots, for two families: the
two-bridge knots and the $(3,n)$-torus knots. For a two-bridge knot $\bp(p,q)$ we give a short,
self-contained proof that every irreducible trace-free $\SU$ representation is binary-dihedral; these
are the $(p-1)/2$ dihedral characters at meridian angles $\cos(2\pi k/p)$, independent of $q$, and the
trace-free Riley polynomial is the explicit product $\phi_p(u)=\prod_k\bigl(u+4\sin^2(\pi k/p)\bigr)$,
monic of degree $(p-1)/2$ with constant term $\det K$. We use this to give a transparent account of the
Hedden--Herald--Kirk theorem that pillowcase homology equals reduced singular instanton knot homology
$\Inat$ on this family, and of why the figure-eight bubbling and bounding cochains that obstruct the
general conjecture are structurally inert there. For the $(3,n)$-torus knots we compute the full
trace-free character variety and prove a dihedral dichotomy: exactly $(\det-1)/2$ characters are
dihedral, so for $n$ odd every irreducible trace-free character is \emph{non}-dihedral. Passing to the
double branched cover $\Sig(2,3,n)$, we self-compute the $\Z/4$ spectral-flow gradings of the generators
from the Fintushel--Stern index and the equivariant $\rho$-invariant, calibrated against the
Poudel--Saveliev and Anvari computations; for $n$ odd the gradings split evenly between $1$ and $3$,
giving the singular-instanton \emph{chain} complex $IC^\natural(T(3,n))=(1+a,a,a,a)$, $a=-\sigma/4$. The
\emph{homology} $\Inat$ equals this for $n\equiv1\pmod6$ (differential zero) but is smaller by $2$ for
$n\equiv5$, where the differential is nonzero: $\operatorname{rank}\Inat=\sum_i|\Delta_{T(3,n)}|$
throughout, and $T(3,5)=P(-2,3,5)=10_{124}$ has rank $7$, not $9$. Finally we reproduce the first
nonzero pillowcase differential, for $8_{19}=T(3,4)$, and identify it as the corner figure-eight bigon
that is absent on two-bridge knots. Throughout we distinguish carefully between results proved here,
classical facts, and computations reproduced from the literature.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{The Atiyah--Floer conjecture and its knot version}
Let $Y$ be a closed oriented $3$-manifold with a Heegaard splitting $Y=Y_0\cup_\Sigma Y_1$. Two Floer
theories attach to this picture. On the gauge-theoretic side is Floer's instanton homology
$HF^{\mathrm{inst}}(Y)$, generated by flat $\SU$ connections and with a differential counting
anti-self-dual instantons on $Y\times\R$. On the symplectic side, the moduli space $M(\Sigma)$ of flat
$\SU$ connections on $\Sigma$ is symplectic, the handlebodies determine Lagrangians $L_0,L_1\subset
M(\Sigma)$, and one forms the Lagrangian Floer homology $HF^{\mathrm{symp}}(L_0,L_1)$. The Atiyah--Floer
conjecture~\cite{Atiyah} asserts these are isomorphic. The one general theorem is the mapping-torus case
of Dostoglou--Salamon~\cite{DS}; the Heegaard-splitting statement remains open, obstructed by the
singularities of $M(\Sigma)$ and by figure-eight bubbling in the adiabatic limit.

There is a knot-theoretic analogue in which the pillowcase plays the role of $M(\Sigma)$. Let
$K\subset S^3$ be a knot, and cut $S^3$ along a Conway sphere meeting $K$ in four points into two
$2$-string tangles. A representation $\rho\colon\pi_1(S^3\setminus K)\to\SU$ is \emph{trace-free} if every
meridian maps to a trace-free element (conjugate to $\operatorname{diag}(i,-i)$); this is the boundary
condition of Kronheimer--Mrowka's singular instanton theory~\cite{KM1,KM2}. Restricting trace-free flat
connections of each tangle to the four-punctured sphere and applying holonomy perturbations, one obtains
immersed Lagrangians $L_0,L_1$ in the \emph{pillowcase}
\[
\pill=(\R/2\pi\Z)^2/\iota,\qquad \iota(\gamma,\theta)=(-\gamma,-\theta),
\]
the traceless $\SU$ character variety of the four-punctured sphere: a two-sphere with four $\Z/2$
orbifold points~\cite{Lin,HHK1}. The \emph{knot Atiyah--Floer conjecture} of Hedden--Herald--Kirk and
Cazassus--Herald--Kirk--Kotelskiy~\cite{HHK1,CHKK} asserts that a bounding-cochain-deformed Lagrangian
Floer homology of $(L_0,L_1)$ is isomorphic, as a relatively $\Z/4$-graded group, to the reduced
singular instanton knot homology $\Inat(K)$.

\subsection{Purpose and scope}
This paper is a computational and expository consolidation. Its aim is to make explicit, for two
tractable infinite families, the representation-theoretic data on which the pillowcase construction
rests: the trace-free character variety (the generators), the $\Z/4$ spectral-flow gradings, and the
differential. We are careful throughout to separate three kinds of statement:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] results proved here (self-contained, though some are surely classical);
\item[(ii)] classical facts and standing theorems (attributed);
\item[(iii)] computations reproduced from~\cite{HHK2,PS,Anvari} (attributed, and independently checked
where possible).
\end{itemize}
We do \emph{not} claim any new theorem about the knot Atiyah--Floer conjecture itself: for two-bridge
knots it is a theorem of Hedden--Herald--Kirk~\cite{HHK1,HHK2}, and for the torus knots we treat the
instanton side and the character-variety side, not the analytic identification of the two. The explicit
verifications reported below---the exact-arithmetic check of Theorem~\ref{thm:A}, the counts of
Proposition~\ref{prop:count}, and the gradings of Section~\ref{sec:grading}---are carried out by a short
computer computation included with the source.

\subsection{Results}
Section~\ref{sec:pillow} records the pillowcase and the trace-free characters as generators.
Section~\ref{sec:2bridge} proves:

\begin{theorem}[Dihedral rigidity for two-bridge knots]\label{thm:A}
Let $K=\bp(p,q)$ be a two-bridge knot, $p=\det K$. Every irreducible trace-free $\SU$ representation of
$\pi_1(S^3\setminus K)$ is binary-dihedral. There are exactly $(p-1)/2$ of them, at meridian-pair angles
$\cos\omega=\cos(2\pi k/p)$ for $k=1,\dots,(p-1)/2$, independent of $q$; equivalently the trace-free
Riley polynomial in $u=2\cos\omega-2$ is
\[
\phi_p(u)=\prod_{k=1}^{(p-1)/2}\Bigl(u+4\sin^2\tfrac{\pi k}{p}\Bigr),
\]
monic of degree $(p-1)/2$, with integer coefficients, $\phi_p(0)=p=\det K$, and root-sum $-p$.
\end{theorem}

We then explain (Section~\ref{sec:2bridge-pillow}) how Theorem~\ref{thm:A} clarifies the
Hedden--Herald--Kirk two-bridge isomorphism and localizes the obstruction, and we correct a naive
expectation about the earring correspondence.

Section~\ref{sec:3n} computes the $(3,n)$-torus knot characters and proves:

\begin{theorem}[Dihedral dichotomy for $(3,n)$-torus knots]\label{thm:B}
Let $\gcd(3,n)=1$. The irreducible trace-free $\SU$ characters of $T(3,n)$ are in bijection with the
admissible representation arcs of Section~\ref{sec:3n}, one per arc. Exactly $(\det-1)/2$ of them are
binary-dihedral: one if $n$ is even $(\det=3)$ and none if $n$ is odd $(\det=1)$. In particular, for $n$
odd every irreducible trace-free character of $T(3,n)$ is non-dihedral.
\end{theorem}

Section~\ref{sec:grading} passes to the double branched cover $\Sig(2,3,n)$ and self-computes the
$\Z/4$ gradings, obtaining for $n$ odd the chain complex $IC^\natural(T(3,n))=(1+a,a,a,a)$, $a=-\sigma/4$,
whose homology $\Inat$ equals it for $n\equiv1\pmod6$ but is smaller by $2$ for $n\equiv5$ (where the
differential is nonzero), the even $\mu\in\{1,3\}$ split accounting for the chain
(Proposition~\ref{prop:split}). Section~\ref{sec:819} reproduces the $8_{19}$ differential and
identifies its geometric source. Section~\ref{sec:open} states the genuinely open direction.

\section{The pillowcase and trace-free characters}\label{sec:pillow}

We work with $\SU$ as the unit quaternions. An element is trace-free iff it is a purely imaginary unit
quaternion (eigenvalues $\pm i$, squaring to $-1$); as a rotation it is a rotation by $\pi$. The
trace-free elements form a $2$-sphere $S\subset\operatorname{Im}\HH\cong\R^3$.

\begin{proposition}[Classical; cf.~\cite{Lin,HHK1}]\label{prop:pillow}
The traceless $\SU$ character variety of the four-punctured sphere is the pillowcase
$\pill=(\R/2\pi\Z)^2/\iota$, with four $\Z/2$ orbifold points at $(\gamma,\theta)\in\{0,\pi\}^2$ and the
symplectic form descending from $d\gamma\wedge d\theta$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Write $\pi_1$ of the four-punctured sphere as
$\langle X_1,\dots,X_4\mid X_1X_2X_3X_4=1\rangle$ with each $X_i\in S$. Since purely imaginary units
satisfy $X_i^{-1}=-X_i$, the relation gives $X_1X_2=X_4X_3=:A$. For pure imaginary units,
$X_iX_j=-\langle X_i,X_j\rangle+X_i\times X_j$, so when $A\neq\pm1$ its axis is well defined; equating the
two expressions for $A$ forces the four $X_i$ to be coplanar, orthogonal to $\operatorname{axis}(A)$.
Placing that axis along $k$ and writing $X_m=\cos\beta_m\,i+\sin\beta_m\,j$, the relation becomes
$\beta_2-\beta_1=\beta_3-\beta_4$; gauge-fixing $\beta_1=0$ and setting $\gamma=\beta_2$,
$\theta=\beta_4$ leaves two parameters, and the residual conjugation by the $\pi$-rotation about $i$ acts
by $(\gamma,\theta)\mapsto(-\gamma,-\theta)$. The four fixed points are the reducible (collinear)
characters.
\end{proof}

For a knot $K$ with a two-tangle decomposition, a representation of $\pi_1(S^3\setminus K)$ is a
compatible pair of tangle representations, i.e.\ a point of $L_0\cap L_1$. Thus the intersection points
are exactly the trace-free representations of the knot group, and (after perturbation) the generators of
the singular instanton chain complex~\cite{KM1,KM2}. In what follows a representation is determined up to
conjugacy by the single invariant $\cos\omega=\langle a,b\rangle$, the cosine of the angle between the
axes of two chosen meridians $a,b\in S$.

\section{Two-bridge knots}\label{sec:2bridge}

\subsection{Trace-free characters are dihedral}
Recall Riley's parametrization~\cite{Riley}: for a two-bridge knot the nonabelian $\mathrm{SL}_2(\C)$
representations with meridian eigenvalue $s$ are
\[
\rho(a)=\begin{pmatrix}s&1\\0&s^{-1}\end{pmatrix},\qquad
\rho(b)=\begin{pmatrix}s&0\\-u&s^{-1}\end{pmatrix},
\]
cut out by a polynomial $\Phi_{p,q}(s,u)$ of degree $(p-1)/2$ in $u$; the trace-free locus is $s=i$.

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:A}]
A binary-dihedral representation sends meridians into the reflection coset $e^{i\phi}j$ of
$\Pin=S^1\cup jS^1$, whose elements are trace-free; hence every irreducible dihedral representation is
trace-free, so there are \emph{at least} $(p-1)/2$ irreducible trace-free characters (the number of
irreducible metabelian representations of a knot with $|H_1(\Sigma_2)|=p$ odd). On the other hand the
trace-free Riley polynomial $\Phi_{p,q}(i,u)$ has $u$-degree at most $(p-1)/2$, so there are \emph{at
most} $(p-1)/2$. The bounds coincide, so every irreducible trace-free character is one of the dihedral
ones. A dihedral character with $\rho(ab)$ of rotation angle $4\pi k/p$ has
$\cos\omega=\cos(2\pi k/p)$; distinct $k\in\{1,\dots,(p-1)/2\}$ give distinct characters, and these
values depend only on $p$. Finally $\phi_p(u)=\prod_k(u-u_k)$ with
$u_k=2\cos(2\pi k/p)-2=-4\sin^2(\pi k/p)$; its constant term is
$\prod_k 4\sin^2(\pi k/p)=p$ and its root-sum is $\sum_k(2\cos(2\pi k/p)-2)=-1-(p-1)=-p$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Small cases: $\phi_3=u+3$, $\phi_5=u^2+5u+5$, $\phi_7=u^3+7u^2+14u+7$, $\phi_9=u^4+9u^3+27u^2+30u+9$. For
$p$ prime, $\phi_p$ is, after $u=2c-2$, the minimal polynomial of $2\cos(2\pi/p)$, irreducible of degree
$(p-1)/2$. Theorem~\ref{thm:A} is elementary and is essentially classical (the trace-free/metabelian
coincidence for two-bridge knots is implicit in~\cite{Riley}); we include the proof because it fixes the
interior generators exactly and $q$-independently, which is what the pillowcase picture needs. The
statement was verified by exact Gaussian-integer computation of the Riley word for all $p\le 9$,
including the two determinant-$9$ knots $9_1=\bp(9,1)$ and the hyperbolic $6_1=\bp(9,2)$, which produce
the identical root set $\{-4\sin^2(\pi k/9)\}$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The pillowcase consequence and the earring}\label{sec:2bridge-pillow}
The character variety of a rational tangle of slope $r$ is a straight arc of slope $r$ in the pillowcase,
and the double branched cover of the four-punctured sphere is a torus $T^2$ on which the tangles lift to
straight lines~\cite{Lin,HHK1,CHKK}. For $\bp(p,q)$ the two lifted lines have classes $(0,1)$ and
$(p,q)$; they meet in $|\det\left(\begin{smallmatrix}0&1\\p&q\end{smallmatrix}\right)|=p$ points, and
gluing the two solid tori recovers $\Sigma_2(K)=L(p,q)$. Being straight lines, they bound no immersed
bigon.

Two things follow, and one warning. First, the naive count on $\pill$ itself is $(p+1)/2$: the $(p-1)/2$
interior dihedral characters of Theorem~\ref{thm:A} together with the single abelian character at a
corner. The passage to the full $\det=p$ requires the reduction defining $\Inat$, i.e.\ the earring.
Second, and structurally: the differential and the bounding-cochain deformation are assembled from
holomorphic bigons and discs, of which straight lines have none. Thus on the two-bridge family the
figure-eight bubbling that obstructs the general conjecture is inert.

This is consistent with the known outcome: Hedden--Herald--Kirk~\cite{HHK1,HHK2} prove that for every
two-bridge knot the $\Z/4$ gradings agree and the differentials vanish on both sides, so pillowcase
homology is isomorphic to $\Inat$ with zero bounding cochain, of rank $\det$. We emphasize this is
\emph{their} theorem; Theorem~\ref{thm:A} only clarifies why the interior generators are exactly the
dihedral characters and why the obstruction is corner-localized.

\begin{remark}[A correction]\label{rmk:earring}
It is tempting to hope that composing the straight Lagrangians with the Cazassus--Herald--Kirk--Kotelskiy
earring correspondence $E\subset\pill^-\times\pill$~\cite{CHKK} preserves straightness. It does not:
Herald--Kirk~\cite{HK} prove, at the regular-homotopy level, that $E$ sends an arc-type Lagrangian to a
genuine figure-eight curve with one self-crossing per corner, while doubling loop-type curves that avoid
the corners. Thus $E\circ L_1$ is immersed, and all of its new geometry is confined to the four corners.
For two-bridge knots the net effect is nonetheless trivial (zero bounding cochain suffices), and every
known example with nonzero bounding cochain---the $(4,5)$-torus knot~\cite{CHKK} and the pretzel knot
$P(-2,3,5)$~\cite{Smith}---is non-alternating.
\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$(3,n)$}{(3,n)}-torus knots}\label{sec:3n}

\subsection{The representation arcs}
Write $\pi_1(S^3\setminus T(3,n))=\langle x,y\mid x^3=y^n\rangle$ with $\gcd(3,n)=1$. The element
$z=x^3=y^n$ is central, so any irreducible $\SU$ representation sends $z\mapsto\pm1$ and
\[
\rho(x)=e^{\alpha\hat u},\quad\rho(y)=e^{\beta\hat v},\qquad
\alpha=\tfrac{\pi\ell_1}{3},\ \ \beta=\tfrac{\pi\ell_2}{n},
\]
with $\ell_1\in\{1,2\}$, $0<\ell_2<n$, and the central-sign match $\ell_1\equiv\ell_2\pmod2$. The angle
$\theta=\angle(\hat u,\hat v)\in(0,\pi)$ is the only remaining modulus; each admissible pair
$(\ell_1,\ell_2)$ is a one-parameter arc of irreducible characters (Klassen~\cite{Klassen}).

\subsection{The trace-free locus}
The meridian is $\mu=x^ay^b$ with $an+3b=1$; it is well defined modulo the center, and trace-freeness is
insensitive to that ambiguity (multiplying $\mu$ by $z$ negates $\rho(\mu)$, preserving trace $0$). A
direct quaternion computation gives
\[
\tfrac12\tr\rho(\mu)=\operatorname{Re}\rho(\mu)
=\cos(a\alpha)\cos(b\beta)-\sin(a\alpha)\sin(b\beta)\cos\theta,
\]
which is affine in $\cos\theta$. Hence each arc contains exactly one trace-free character, at
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tf}
\cos\theta=\cot(a\alpha)\,\cot(b\beta),
\end{equation}
provided the right side lies in $[-1,1]$ (otherwise the arc carries none). We verified in each case that
the resulting $\rho$ satisfies $\rho(x)^3=\rho(y)^n=-1$ and $\tr\rho(\mu)=0$ exactly.

\begin{proposition}[Count]\label{prop:count}
The number of irreducible trace-free $\SU$ characters of $T(3,n)$ is
\[
N(3,n)=\begin{cases}
2(n-1)/3,& n\equiv1,\\ (2n-1)/3,& n\equiv2,\\ (2n+1)/3,& n\equiv4,\\ 2(n+1)/3,& n\equiv5,
\end{cases}\pmod 6,
\]
together with one abelian trace-free character; thus $N(3,n)\sim 2n/3$.
\end{proposition}

The values $N(3,n)$ for $n=2,4,5,7,8,10,11,13$ are $1,3,4,4,5,7,8,8$. These count the admissible arcs,
i.e.\ those on which the right side of~\eqref{eq:tf} lies in $[-1,1]$; the formula was checked by direct
enumeration of the admissible arcs for all $n\le 25$.

\subsection{The dihedral dichotomy}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:B}]
In $\Pin=S^1\cup jS^1$ every element is a rotation (in $S^1$) or a reflection (in $jS^1$); reflections
are exactly the trace-free elements, and an irreducible dihedral representation must send a generator to
a reflection. Now $\rho(x)$ has trace $2\cos(\pi\ell_1/3)=\pm1\neq0$, so it is never trace-free (as $3$
is odd); and $\rho(y)$ is trace-free iff $\beta=\pi/2$ iff $\ell_2=n/2$, possible only for $n$ even. Hence
the number of irreducible dihedral trace-free characters is $1$ if $n$ is even and $0$ if $n$ is odd,
which equals $(\det T(3,n)-1)/2$ since $\det T(3,n)=3$ for $n$ even and $1$ for $n$ odd. In particular,
for $n$ odd there are no dihedral characters, so every irreducible trace-free character is non-dihedral.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:B} is the exact opposite of Theorem~\ref{thm:A}: two-bridge knots are entirely
dihedral, the $(3,n)$-torus knots with $n$ odd entirely non-dihedral. The non-dihedral characters are
precisely those the pillowcase construction represents by non-straight Lagrangians, on which the earring
produces genuine figure-eight curves; this is why the family is a natural first test beyond the
metabelian world.

\begin{remark}
There is a visible symmetry: $(\ell_1,\ell_2)\mapsto(3-\ell_1,\,n-\ell_2)$ preserves $\theta$
in~\eqref{eq:tf}, pairing the two central-sign sheets $\rho(x)^3=\pm1$ at equal meridian angle.
\end{remark}

\section{The \texorpdfstring{$\Z/4$}{Z/4} gradings via the double branched cover}\label{sec:grading}

The double branched cover of $T(3,n)$ is the Brieskorn sphere $\Sig(2,3,n)$. For $n$ odd it is an
integral homology sphere; its irreducible flat $\SO$ connections have rotation numbers
$(m_1,m_2,m_3)=(1,m_2,m_3)$ indexing the three singular fibers, with $m_2,m_3$ even, $0<m_2<3$,
$0<m_3<n$. Since $2$ is the only even value in $(0,3)$, necessarily $m_2=2$, and $m_3$ ranges over the
even integers in $(0,n)$ meeting the spherical-triangle inequalities on the angles
$(\pi/2,2\pi/3,\pi m_3/n)$. (These $m_i$ index the branched cover and are not the $(\ell_1,\ell_2)$ of
Section~\ref{sec:3n}.) Their number is $a=-\sigma(T(3,n))/4$, and each lifts to two trace-free knot
characters, so
\begin{equation}\label{eq:N2a}
N(3,n)=2a\qquad(n\text{ odd}),\qquad \sigma(T(3,n))=-2N(3,n),
\end{equation}
which we verified (independent triangle-inequality count) for all odd $n\le43$. Thus the character count
of Proposition~\ref{prop:count} determines the signature and, with the grading formulae below, the
graded homology.

\subsection{The grading formula}
The $\Z/4$ grading is the spectral flow of the extended Hessian mod $4$, relative to the trivial
connection $\Theta$ (a single generator, in grading $\sigma\bmod4$); every irreducible contributes four
generators, two in grading $\mu$ and two in $\mu+1$~\cite{KM2,PS}. The knot grading is
\[
\mu(\alpha)=\tfrac12\gr(\alpha)+\tfrac14\bigl(1-\rho_{\mathrm{Ad}\,\alpha}\bigr)\pmod 4,
\]
where, for $\Sig(2,p,q)$ with $p=3$, $q=n$, the Fintushel--Stern index~\cite{FS,Anvari} and the
equivariant $\rho$-invariant~\cite{Anvari} are
\begin{align}
\gr(\alpha)&=\frac{e^2}{pq}+\frac2p\sum_{k=1}^{p-1}\cot\tfrac{\pi k}{p}\cot\tfrac{\pi b_2 k}{p}
\sin^2\tfrac{\pi k m_2}{p}+\frac2q\sum_{k=1}^{q-1}\cot\tfrac{\pi k}{q}\cot\tfrac{\pi b_3 k}{q}
\sin^2\tfrac{\pi k m_3}{q},\label{eq:FS}\\
\rho_{\mathrm{Ad}\,\alpha}&=1-\frac{2}{pq}-\frac4p\sum_{k=1}^{p-1}\cot\tfrac{\pi k}{p}\tan\tfrac{\pi b_2 k}{p}
\cos^2\tfrac{\pi k m_2}{p}-\frac4q\sum_{k=1}^{q-1}\cot\tfrac{\pi k}{q}\tan\tfrac{\pi b_3 k}{q}
\cos^2\tfrac{\pi k m_3}{q},\label{eq:rho}
\end{align}
with $e=pq\,m_1+2q\,m_2+2p\,m_3$ and $b_2,b_3$ the even Seifert invariants
($pqb_1+2qb_2+2pb_3=1$); since the cotangents see them only mod $p,q$, one may take
$b_2\equiv(2q)^{-1}$, $b_3\equiv(2p)^{-1}$.

\begin{example}\label{ex:237}
For $\Sig(2,3,7)$ the two connections $(1,2,2)$ and $(1,2,4)$ give
$\gr=175\equiv7$ and $\gr=251\equiv3\pmod8$, $\rho=3$ in both cases, and
$\mu=87\equiv3$ and $\mu=125\equiv1\pmod4$. This reproduces Anvari's Example~6.1~\cite{Anvari} to the raw
values, and calibrates our implementation.
\end{example}

\subsection{The gradings of $T(3,n)$, $n$ odd}
Evaluating~\eqref{eq:FS}--\eqref{eq:rho} over the admissible connections gives the following.

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:split}
Let $n$ be odd and coprime to $3$, and set $a=-\sigma(T(3,n))/4=N(3,n)/2$ (an even integer). Under the
grading~\eqref{eq:FS} the $a$ irreducible connections of $\Sig(2,3,n)$ split evenly: $a/2$ have $\mu=1$
and $a/2$ have $\mu=3$ (never $0$ or $2$). Consequently the reduced singular-instanton \emph{chain}
complex is
\[
IC^\natural(T(3,n))=(1+a,\,a,\,a,\,a),\qquad \chi=+1,
\]
with the trivial generator $\Theta$ in grading $0$. This is the Poudel--Saveliev chain-level
statement~\cite[\S7.4]{PS} (Anvari~\cite{Anvari} computes exactly these generators and gradings; the
irreducible part is Daemi--Scaduto's~\cite{DScaduto}). The \emph{homology} $\Inat$ then splits by
$n\bmod 6$:
\[
\operatorname{rank}\Inat(T(3,n))=\sum_i\bigl|\Delta_{T(3,n)}\bigr|=
\begin{cases}1+4a, & n\equiv1\pmod 6\quad(\partial=0),\\[2pt]
4a-1=(1+4a)-2, & n\equiv5\pmod 6\quad(\operatorname{rank}\partial=1).\end{cases}
\]
For $n\equiv1$ the Alexander lower bound $\sum_i|\Delta|=1+4a$ meets the chain rank, forcing $\partial=0$
and $\Inat=IC^\natural=(1+a,a,a,a)$ (Anvari~\cite[Ex.~6.2]{Anvari} proves this family). For $n\equiv5$ the
differential is nonzero; the smallest case is $T(3,5)=P(-2,3,5)=10_{124}$, where $\operatorname{rank}\Inat
=7$ (not $9$), pinned by $\ell=u=7$~\cite{Smith}, and where on the pillowcase side the nonzero bounding
cochain of Smith~\cite{Smith} enacts the cancellation.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof and verification]
On $\Sig(2,3,n)$ one has $\rho\equiv3$, so $\mu=(\gr-1)/2$. A $\mu=3$ connection contributes two chain
generators to each of gradings $3$ and $0$; a $\mu=1$ connection two to each of $1$ and $2$. That $a$ is
even follows from $a=(n-1)/3=2m$ for $n=6m+1$ and $a=(n+1)/3=2m+2$ for $n=6m+5$; so with $a/2$ of each
type the irreducibles give $(a,a,a,a)$, and $\Theta$ adds one to grading $0$, giving
$IC^\natural=(1+a,a,a,a)$ of chain rank $1+4a$. The even split (hence this chain complex) is proved for
$n\equiv1\pmod6$ by Anvari~\cite[Ex.~6.2]{Anvari} and verified here by direct evaluation
of~\eqref{eq:FS}--\eqref{eq:rho} for all odd $n\le43$. For the homology, $\sum_i|\Delta_{T(3,n)}|=1+4a$
when $n\equiv1$ and $4a-1$ when $n\equiv5$; since the Khovanov--Alexander bounds give
$\sum_i|\Delta|\le\operatorname{rank}\Inat\le\operatorname{rank}\widetilde{Kh}$ and, for these knots,
$\operatorname{rank}\widetilde{Kh}=\sum_i|\Delta|$, the differential vanishes iff $n\equiv1$; for
$n\equiv5$ it has rank $1$. (The case $T(3,5)$ is Smith's~\cite{Smith}, rigorous with $\ell=u=7$.)
\end{proof}

Explicitly $a=(n-1)/3$ for $n\equiv1$ and $a=(n+1)/3$ for $n\equiv5\pmod6$; either way
$\operatorname{rank}\Inat\sim 2N(3,n)$ grows while $\det=1$ is fixed, so $\Inat(T(3,n))$ is far from thin.
We note that $\chi(\Inat(K))=+1$ for \emph{every} knot~\cite[Thm~8.1]{PS}---carried by the single trivial
generator---so here $\chi$ coincides with $\det=1$ only by accident; in general $\chi\neq\det$, as the
non-thin case $8_{19}$ below ($\chi=1$, $\det=3$) shows.

The Fintushel--Stern grading is special to $n$ odd, where $\Sig(2,3,n)$ is a homology sphere; for $n$
even $b_3\equiv(2p)^{-1}\bmod n$ does not exist. A nonzero differential appears both for $n\equiv5\pmod6$
(above) and for $n$ even. The smallest and cleanest instance---bounding cochain vanishing, differential a
single bigon---is $8_{19}=T(3,4)$, to which we now turn.

\section{A nonzero differential: \texorpdfstring{$8_{19}=T(3,4)$}{8\_19 = T(3,4)}}\label{sec:819}

For $n$ even the double cover $\Sig(2,3,n)$ carries nontrivial reducibles ($H_1\neq0$), and the pillowcase
complex acquires a differential. We treat the smallest case, $8_{19}=T(3,4)$, in two ways.

\subsection{Structural derivation}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:B} and Proposition~\ref{prop:count}, $T(3,4)$ has three irreducible trace-free
characters---two non-dihedral (at $\theta\approx125.3^\circ,54.7^\circ$) and one dihedral (at $90^\circ$)
---together with one abelian character. Via the double cover $\Sig(2,3,4)$, whose first homology is
$\Z/3$: the two non-dihedral characters are the two lifts of the single $\Sig$-irreducible; the dihedral
character is the nontrivial reducible; the abelian character is $\Theta$. Through the grading framework
(irreducible $\to$ four generators at $(\mu,\mu,\mu{+}1,\mu{+}1)$, nontrivial reducible $\to$ two,
$\Theta\to$ one at $\sigma\bmod4=2$), with $\mu_{\mathrm{irr}}=3$ and $\mu_{\mathrm{red}}=1$, the chain
complex assembles to
\[
C^\natural=(2,1,2,2),\qquad \chi=+1,
\]
matching the instanton computation of Poudel--Saveliev~\cite[Ex.~7.9]{PS}. A finite search over
grading-respecting differentials shows that the \emph{only} differential compatible with homology
$(2,1,1,1)$ is a single rank-one map $\partial_3\colon C_3\to C_2$: one bigon from a grading-$3$
generator (a lift of the irreducible, an interior point) to a grading-$2$ generator (the
reducible/$\Theta$, a corner point). We derived this before consulting~\cite{HHK2}; it predicts an
interior-to-corner bigon.

\subsection{The explicit bigon}
Hedden--Herald--Kirk~\cite[\S11.6]{HHK2} realize exactly this. With $L_0$ the earring figure-eight and
$L_1$ the perturbed character variety of the torus-knot tangle $(p,q,r,s)=(3,4,3,-2)$, the singular
unperturbed variety (a ``$\varphi$-curve'') resolves into an arc $R_0$ and a vertically monotone circle
$R_1$. The seven generators are $r_+$ (corner, $\gr=2$), $x_1^\pm$ (arc), and $x_2^\pm,x_3^\pm$ (circle);
the gradings are $\gr(x_1^+)=0$, $\gr(x_1^-)=3$, and the circle contributes one generator in each
grading. There is exactly one bigon, $x_1^-\to r_+$, running into the corner along $R_0$ and the earring,
giving $\partial x_1^-=r_+$---a rank-one map from grading $3$ to grading $2$. The circle bounds no bigon.
The homology is
\[
H^\natural=(1,0,0,0)_{\text{arc}}\oplus(1,1,1,1)_{\text{circle}}=(2,1,1,1)=\Inat(8_{19}),
\]
of rank $5>\det=3$; the complex is non-thin.

\subsection{The corner-bigon thread}
The structural derivation and the explicit computation give the same graded complex and the same
differential. The bigon is a corner figure-eight bigon---the earring's self-crossing at a pillowcase
corner linking an interior irreducible character to the reducible locus. This is precisely the mechanism
absent on two-bridge knots (Section~\ref{sec:2bridge-pillow}): there the Lagrangians are straight, there
is no corner bigon, and $\partial=0$; here the single corner bigon is what makes $\Inat(8_{19})$
non-thin. The geometric computation is due to~\cite{HHK2}; our contribution is the independent structural
derivation predicting the differential's rank, source, and target from the character count alone.

\section{The open direction}\label{sec:open}

Everything above concerns generators and gradings; the analytic heart of the knot Atiyah--Floer
conjecture is the bounding cochain. On two-bridge knots it is inert (Section~\ref{sec:2bridge-pillow});
off them it is genuinely nonzero, as shown for the $(4,5)$-torus knot~\cite{CHKK} and the pretzel knot
$P(-2,3,5)$~\cite{Smith}. Notably $P(-2,3,5)=T(3,5)=10_{124}$ is itself a member of our family---the
smallest $n\equiv5\pmod6$ case---so the nonzero differential of Proposition~\ref{prop:split} for
$n\equiv5$ is, on the pillowcase side, precisely Smith's nonzero bounding cochain. A differential thus
appears for $n\equiv5$ (odd) and for all $n$ even; two problems, of increasing difficulty, remain.

\emph{First, the instanton side.} For every torus knot the irreducible singular instanton homology is
$\Z_{(1)}^{\lceil-\sigma/4\rceil}\oplus\Z_{(3)}^{\lfloor-\sigma/4\rfloor}$ with vanishing
differential~\cite{DScaduto}. For $n$ even, however, $\Sig(2,3,n)$ carries a nontrivial reducible
($\det=3$, so $H_1=\Z/3$), and the reduced complex has the shape
\[
C^\natural(T(3,n))\ =\ \underbrace{\tfrac{N-1}{2}\ \text{irreducibles}}_{4\ \text{generators each}}
\ \oplus\ \underbrace{\text{one reducible}}_{2}\ \oplus\ \Theta,\qquad \dim=2N(3,n)+1,
\]
on which an irreducible-to-reducible differential can appear---exactly the corner figure-eight bigon of
$8_{19}$ (Section~\ref{sec:819}). Organizing this differential across the even family---in particular,
whether it is always of rank one, as for $8_{19}$, giving $\Inat$ of rank $2N(3,n)-1$---is open beyond
the single case $n=4$~\cite{PS}.

\emph{Second, and harder, the pillowcase side:} to compute the bounding-cochain-deformed pillowcase
invariant and match it to $\Inat$. No bounding cochain has been computed for an infinite family in any
setting; the record is the $(4,5)$-torus knot (correct rank~\cite{CHKK}) and $P(-2,3,5)$ (nonzero
only~\cite{Smith}). The immersed-curve machinery of~\cite{HK} is the natural tool, but its assembly into
a family computation is not yet in place. Both problems lie beyond the present, purely
representation-theoretic, account.
